Before you start programming, you should install IntelliJ IDEA on your computer. This will make programming much easier and enjoyable! Simply choose whether you're a Windows PC or a Mac and instructions will appear. Good luck!

Mac Instructions

In order to code in Java, first of all you need a development kit named JDK (Java Development Kit).
In short, JDK is a tool used by your computer in order to translate your code into a language that the brain of your computer understands.:)
To install JDK for your mac, click here, you will be redirected to the website below.

click on the "Accept License Agreement" in the middle of the page, and then click on the MAC OS X link in the middle of the table.
Once you downloaded the JDK image, click on it. You will a window like the image bellow.
Click on the box, follow the instructions (Meaning just click Continue in each section!) and wait for it to install.
Yo! you're now one step ahead to do Java!

Now it's the time to install the environment that you are going to work with! the IntelliJ!
First thing to do in order to install intelliJ on your Mac, is to jump to the JetBrain Website and download IntelliJ!
Simply click here to Automatically download it :P. Once you download it, click on it, you'll face the image bellow, just drag and drop the IntelliJ icon to your applications.

after doing so, you can open IntelliJ from your applications! First choose the theme you'd like (Personally I would go for Dracula, Dark Wins :P!)

continue each step and ignore what you see (Those are for proffesional users who wanna work in other languages)
Congtrats!!! You're done with the installation!

Now you can make your project in order to work on your code! when you open IntelliJ, you'll first face the image bellow:

Create a new Project, you will be redirected to the page below. Here you have to add the JDK that you downloaded.

in order to do that, you have to first click on the "New" button on the right uper side of the screen, and then choose the path of your JDK,
which means you need to choose where ypu have installed all the JDK stuff. (Usually IntelliJ itself detects where it has been installed.)

After This Stage, You will See another Window, Asking for your project name first. Choose a coo Project name such as "Hello Java"
Keep in mind to delete the Package section and keep it blank.
Cick continue, and magic happens!!! You will face the image below, where you can actually write your code in!
